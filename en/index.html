03 · Product strategy

The back-office is the central piece

Product ambition doesn't live on the front-end — it lives on the admin side. That's what will truly set this project apart from agency-locked platforms, more than the graphic layer.

Accessible back-office02:05

  • Admin system where UBLOT manages tree and content in real time.
  • Nathan would like to be able to add festival landing pages or one-off event pages — to challenge on technical feasibility on our side.
  • Additions handled in-house — no agency budget needed for each change.
  • Expected impact: lower costs for UBLOT, faster turnaround on communication.

Visual simplicity & structure05:50

  • Mobile-first — home page with a photo slider/carousel + agenda grid or carousel.
  • Clear split between concerts and clubbing for readability.
  • Ticketing via Shotgun — API used for customisation without adding complexity.
  • AD steered by Nathan and Studio bestiole — simple but effective consistency.

Flexibility & evolution23:17

  • UBLOT can disable temporary pages (e.g. a one-off tasting event) without heavy intervention.
  • Specific modifications handled within 48h, no extra fees.
  • Balance UBLOT autonomy / fast technical support, without inflating the budget.
  • Simple V1 deployed fast; more advanced V2 considered later.
04 · Design & UX

Minimal but alive

The venue's identity should come through images and videos rather than a heavy layout. Demanding on the content side, but much more sustainable on the code side.

Spare design, identity through image07:30

  • Reference Le Sucre — animated background videos for atmosphere, without excessive complexity.
  • White, simple layout. AD shines through images and embedded multimedia.
  • Intuitive navigation: burger menu, quick agenda access from the home page.
  • Explicit anti-pattern: avoid overloaded sites that "give you a headache."

Fluid & satisfying navigation21:05

  • Scroll animations and intuitive sliders to reduce clicks.
  • Pleasant content discovery, for first-timers and regulars alike.
  • Key animation choices to validate once Nathan's mockup is in.

Mobile / desktop adaptation25:40

  • Automatic device detection to serve adapted content (e.g. a different home page video).
  • Progressive evolution — V1 vs V2 scope locked down as soon as the mockup arrives.
